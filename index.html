Baked Ziti

Ziti is a hollow pasta in the shape of a smooth tube, sort of like a short straw. It originated in Campagna, Italy, or perhaps Sicily, Italy. Its name means bride or bridegroom, although the stories of why the Italians gave this pasta the name for those getting married can vary. In some parts of Italy, it's served at weddings.

Steak & Kidney Pie

Steak and Kidney Pie has deep roots in British culinary history. It is often associated with traditional pub fare and hearty, comforting meals. The combination of meaty, flavorful steak and the unique taste of kidneys creates a dish that has been enjoyed for generations.

Tuna Casserole

The origins of Tuna Casserole can be traced back to mid-20th-century America. With the popularity of canned tuna and convenience-focused cooking during this era, Tuna Casserole became a go-to recipe for busy families. It provided a quick and affordable way to create a wholesome and satisfying one-dish meal.
